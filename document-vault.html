How to Mint a Document

1

Get Your Document Into PDF Form

GameChanger's minting tool requires a PDF file. If your document isn't already a PDF (a Word doc, a note from any text editor, a scan, etc.), use a free converter like freeconvert.com — upload your file, choose PDF as the output, then download the converted file to your computer.

2

Get a Free Pinata Account

Sign up at pinata.cloud (free tier works fine) and generate a JWT API key from your account settings. This is what lets GameChanger permanently store your file.

3

Open NFT Creator in GameChanger

In your GameChanger Wallet, click NFT Creator in the left menu, then go to the Advanced tab.

4

Connect Your Pinata Key

Paste your Pinata JWT token into the Advanced tab and accept. This connects your wallet to your own Pinata storage — your key stays between you and GameChanger.

5

Upload Your Document

Click the mint button, browse for your PDF (BOL, receipt, inspection report, etc.), and fill in a title and description so you can find it later.

6

Set Quantity and Mint

Choose how many copies to mint (usually just 1 for a document), upload, and continue.

7

Sign, Send, and Confirm

Click the NFT, hit sign and send, enter your wallet password, and accept. Make sure you have a small amount of ADA in your wallet to cover the minting fee.

8

Done — It's Permanent

Your document is now minted to your wallet and permanently hosted on Pinata. You can close GameChanger and come back to it anytime — it's not going anywhere.

This isn't hypothetical. I owed the IRS over $15,000 in capital gains tax, and the employee tax forms my business needed to sort it out were paper — and got lost. I scanned what I had left with my phone, converted it to a PDF, minted it through GameChanger, and it was permanently saved on Pinata. ⚠ A Few Things to Know

This process happens entirely inside your own GameChanger Wallet and your own Pinata account — Midnight Loadboard does not see, store, or have access to your documents, your Pinata key, or your wallet password. Never share your Pinata JWT or wallet password with anyone, including us. Minting requires a small amount of ADA in your wallet to cover network fees.
