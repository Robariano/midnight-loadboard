About the Founder

Built From a Real Problem

Warehouse work, bad routing tools, and a detour through bodybuilding NFTs — here's how Midnight Loadboard actually came together.

The Background

My family has run A&L Coors, a beer distribution business, for years — I was in and out of it for over a decade. I had my own delivery routes early on, went to school part-time, stepped back to part-time, left, and came back more than once. The last several years there, I worked mostly in the warehouse, driving about one day a week. I hold a CDL with all endorsements and I'm a Navy veteran.

The Detour That Led Here

I left a good job at Marriott to try driving again — it didn't work out. My last driving job forced us onto Google Maps for routing, and it was genuinely bad for trucks. That frustration sent me down a technical rabbit hole: the Google Maps API, Google's Fleet Engine, even a drone certification class, all while thinking about what a real "Google Maps for truckers" could look like.

Finding Cardano

Somewhere in that research I found Cardano, and a potential partnership angle with Google Cloud, and that's what got me serious about actually building something. I lost my last driving job a little over a year ago — that's ultimately what pushed me to build full-time instead of just thinking about it.

The Bodybuilding Chapter

Before any of this, I was also deep into bodybuilding — I still run @MintYourReps on Twitter/X, built around permanently documenting training progress instead of relying on notebooks or apps that can lose data.

The Project Catalyst ProposalNot Funded

I put a real proposal out on Cardano's Project Catalyst — using daily token minting so bodybuilders could permanently log workout journals instead of paying for cloud storage that could vanish. It didn't get funded, but the core idea stuck: permanent, tamper-proof record-keeping has real value anywhere people need proof of something they did.

Where That Leaves Midnight Loadboard

I'm not currently an active carrier — I don't have equipment or insurance to haul freight myself right now, and I want to be upfront about that. What I am is someone who's spent time on both sides of this industry (warehouse, dispatch-adjacent work, family logistics business) and who ran headfirst into bad routing tools and worse paperwork problems. Midnight Loadboard is what I'm building instead of just complaining about it — permanent documents, verified credentials, and a network I'm building in the open, one real carrier at a time.
