About the Founder

From Gym Journals to BOLs

The permanent record-keeping idea behind Midnight Loadboard didn't start with trucking — it started in a gym.

The Background

I spent 12 years at A&L Coors, my family's beer distribution business, out of Durango, Colorado. I hold a CDL with all endorsements and I'm a Navy veteran.

The Bodybuilding Chapter

Before any of this, my focus was bodybuilding — I still run @MintYourReps on Twitter/X, built around a simple idea: permanently documenting training progress instead of relying on notebooks or apps that can lose data or disappear.

The Project Catalyst ProposalNot Funded

I put a real proposal out on Cardano's Project Catalyst — using daily token minting as a way for bodybuilders to permanently log workout journals instead of paying for cloud storage that could vanish. It didn't get funded, but the core idea stuck with me: permanent, tamper-proof record-keeping has real value anywhere people need proof of something they did.

The Bridge to Trucking

When I got back into the freight side of things, it clicked — drivers deal with the exact same problem I was trying to solve in the gym. A BOL isn't that different from a training log. Both are proof of work you actually did, and both deserve to be protected the same way. That's where Midnight Loadboard's Document Vault came from.
